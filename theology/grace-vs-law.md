### The Difference Between the Law and Grace

**"For the law was given by Moses, but grace and truth came by Jesus Christ."** - John 1:17

The Bible presents two distinct administrations: **the Law** and **Grace**. The Law reveals man's inability and exposes sin, while Grace reveals God's provision and offers righteousness through faith in Christ.

| THE LAW                                                       | THE GRACE                                                        |
| ------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Given by Moses (John 1:17)                                    | Came by Jesus Christ (John 1:17)                                 |
| Written on tables of stone (2 Corinthians 3:3)                | Written on the tables of the heart (2 Corinthians 3:4)           |
| Law of works (Romans 3:27)                                    | Law of faith (Romans 3:27)                                       |
| "The man that doeth them shall live in them" (Galatians 3:12) | "The just shall live by faith" (Galatians 3:11)                  |
| No flesh is justified by the deeds of the law (Romans 3:20)   | Justified freely by His grace (Romans 3:24)                      |
| Gives the knowledge of sin (Romans 3:20)                      | Salvation by grace through faith (Ephesians 2:8)                 |
| Weak and unprofitable to perfect man (Hebrews 7:18)           | Brings a better hope by which we draw near to God (Hebrews 7:19) |

### What the Law Could Do

The Law is holy, just, and good. However, it was never given as a means for fallen humanity to obtain righteousness. Its purpose was to reveal sin and demonstrate mankind's inability to meet God's perfect standard.

> The Law acts like a mirror—it can show the dirt on a person's face, but it cannot wash it away.

### What Grace Accomplishes

Grace does what the Law could never do. Through the death and resurrection of Jesus Christ, God provides righteousness as a gift. Under Grace, believers do not strive to become accepted by God; they live from the acceptance already given in Christ.

> The Law says, "Do and live."
> Grace says, "Believe and live."

### The Great Transition

The movement from Law to Grace is not merely a change of commandments but a change of covenant:

* From external tablets to an inward work of the Spirit.
* From human effort to divine provision.
* From condemnation to justification.
* From distance from God to confident access to God.

### Conclusion

The Law reveals the sinner's need. Grace reveals the Savior.

The Law points to Christ. Grace is fulfilled in Christ.

Therefore, the believer's life is not sustained by self-effort but by faith in the finished work of Jesus Christ, who has provided a **better hope through which we draw near unto God** (Hebrews 7:19).
